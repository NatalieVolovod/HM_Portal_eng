Learning to make candles with your own hands

By adding shavings from colored wax pencils to a homemade candle, a variety of color combinations and effects can be achieved.

In this tutorial, weâ€™ll talk about how to make a candle with a colorful geometric pattern, but you can experiment and try to create your own unique pattern. If you want the colors to move seamlessly into one another, just fill in the next layer, without waiting for the first to dry.

You will need:

  • small glass cups (made of transparent or frosted glass), as well as dishes in which you will heat the wax (paper cups for ice cream are also suitable);
  • wax;
  • wax pencils;
  • wick;
  • wooden sticks for stirring.

  • Fill the cup with wax and put it in the microwave for 1 minute. Put hot wax on the bottom of the glass cup and fix the wick in it. Wait until it freezes.

  • Prepare wax pencils: to do this, remove the paper and cut them into small pieces. Fill one of the cups with wax, put on top pieces of a pencil (of the same color!) And put in the microwave for 2 minutes, then mix to get a homogeneous mass.

  • Take the glass cups, tilt them at an angle of about 300 degrees (you can use the dish dryer as a support) and fill with colored wax (about 1/3). Leave on for 20-30 minutes until the wax has hardened. Turn over the jars and fill the second layer, then wait again until it hardens. Before pouring the last layer, place the jars vertically on the table. Before lighting a candle, wait about an hour until it cools completely.

And you're done!
